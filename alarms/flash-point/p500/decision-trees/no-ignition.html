Decision Tree: No Ignition Detected

Symptom: ignition never occurs, or analyzer does not detect a flash event.

← Back to Trees ← Alarm Codes P-500 Main Page →
Safety: Ensure enclosure closed, ventilation OK, and follow site rules before any ignition tests.

Step-by-step isolation

1) Confirm the correct method/program is selected (closed cup/open cup settings, ramp rate, ignition interval).
2) Verify sample cup is clean, dry, properly seated, and filled to correct volume (no spills on rim or lid seal).
3) Check enclosure door/lid interlock: ensure switches fully engaged (many “no ignition” events are blocked by interlock).
4) Perform ignition check:
  • Inspect ignition electrode/spark tip condition (carbon deposits, damage).
  • Confirm electrode gap and positioning (as per manual).
  • Verify ignition wiring connectors seated and dry.
5) If spark is weak/intermittent:
  • Check HV ignition supply output (if test point available).
  • Inspect cable insulation for cracking or moisture.
  • Verify proper grounding/earthing.
6) If ignition occurs but is not detected:
  • Clean flame/optical sensor window/lens.
  • Check sensor alignment and cable seating.
  • Verify detector status in diagnostics.
7) Final validation:
  • Run a certified reference material to confirm detection and repeatability.
  • If still failing, replace ignition electrode and/or detector module per manual.
