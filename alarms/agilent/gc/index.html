How to use this alarm hub

  1. Identify the alarm or symptom shown on the GC
  2. Confirm utilities and sample conditions first
  3. Follow the recommended field checks in order
  4. Verify recovery using stable chromatograms and trends
Field reality: Most GC alarms are caused by sample system issues, carrier gas instability, moisture, or valve problems — not electronics.

Common Agilent GC Alarm Categories

No Peaks / Missing Peaks

Injection failure, valve issue, restriction, or detector fault.

Poor Repeatability

Pressure instability, leaks, moisture, or valve timing drift.

Retention Time Shift

Carrier flow changes, oven temperature drift, column issues.

Calibration Failure

Standard gas issues, unstable baseline, detector problems.

Detector Fault

TCD/FID power, contamination, electronics, or wiring.

Communication / Data Issues

LAN settings, software mapping, time sync, or data quality flags.
