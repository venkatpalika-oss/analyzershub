Poor Repeatability / High RSD

Consecutive GC runs show inconsistent peak areas, unstable concentrations, or high relative standard deviation (RSD).

← Agilent GC Alarms Agilent GC Overview

What this condition means

Poor repeatability occurs when successive chromatographic runs do not produce consistent peak areas or concentrations under stable process conditions. This almost always indicates instability in sample delivery, carrier gas, or valve operation.

Most Common Causes (Field Priority)

  1. Carrier gas pressure or flow instability
  2. Leaks in sample or carrier gas system
  3. Moisture or liquid carryover in sample
  4. Injection valve wear or sticking
  5. Unstable regulator or failing pressure control

Field Checks (Recommended Order)

  • Trend carrier gas pressure and verify stability
  • Leak test sample and carrier manifolds
  • Inspect sample conditioning for moisture carryover
  • Observe valve switching consistency and timing
  • Review chromatograms for peak shape variability

Corrective Actions

  • Stabilize carrier gas supply and replace faulty regulators
  • Repair leaks in tubing, fittings, or manifolds
  • Improve sample drying and filtration
  • Service or replace worn injection valves
  • Allow sufficient stabilization time after maintenance
Field tip:
If repeatability improves immediately after maintenance but degrades again within hours or days, suspect moisture ingress or a slow carrier leak.

Applicable Agilent GC Models
