No Peaks / Missing Peaks

Analyzer runs but chromatogram shows no peaks or expected components are missing.

← Agilent GC Alarms Agilent GC

What this alarm means

The GC method executes normally, but one or more expected components do not appear on the chromatogram. This usually indicates an injection, flow, or detection problem.

Most Common Causes (Field Priority)

  1. No sample reaching the injection valve
  2. Injection valve not switching or stuck
  3. Sample loop blocked or empty
  4. Detector not powered or disabled
  5. Carrier gas supply missing or incorrect

Field Checks (Recommended Order)

  • Verify sample pressure and flow at GC inlet
  • Observe valve switching timing during analysis
  • Check carrier gas pressure and traps
  • Confirm detector power and baseline signal
  • Inject calibration gas to verify response

Corrective Actions

  • Restore sample flow and remove restrictions
  • Service or replace faulty injection valve
  • Clean or replace blocked sample loop
  • Restore detector power or configuration
  • Correct carrier gas supply and pressure
Field tip: If all peaks disappear suddenly, suspect injection valve failure or carrier gas loss first.

Applicable Agilent GC Models

  • Agilent 8850 GC
  • Agilent 8860 GC
  • Agilent 8890 GC
  • Agilent 990 Micro GC
