GC8000 — Detector Fault / Noise / No Signal (Decision Tree)

Use when detector alarms occur, baseline is noisy, or peaks disappear.

Golden rule: Confirm carrier/sample stability before blaming the detector. Many “detector faults” are actually carrier instability or contamination.
← Back to Yokogawa Trees GC8000 Main Page →

Decision Tree

1) Carrier stable?
If carrier pressure/flow unstable → fix carrier first (see Carrier Pressure Low tree).
2) Baseline noise sudden?
Sudden noise often indicates moisture/contamination or grounding/shielding problems.
3) Electrical checks
Check grounding, shield continuity, loose terminals, power supply stability.
4) Contamination
Check for wet/dirty sample, carryover, failed filters/traps. Contamination affects detector response.
5) Detector module health
Review diagnostics and status values. If detector output flat/no response even with stable conditions → suspect detector electronics.
6) After fix
Run stable cycles and verify repeatability, baseline stability, and expected peak response.

Fast Checks

  • Carrier stability and leaks
  • Sample dryness/conditioning
  • Grounding/shielding and terminal tightness
  • Diagnostics: detector signal level and alarms
