ZR22G — High Drift / Unstable Reading (Decision Tree)

Use this when O₂ gradually moves up/down or becomes unstable after being stable.

Golden rule: Drift is usually from probe contamination, reference air issues, or heater instability — not calibration.
← Back to Yokogawa Trees ZR22G Main Page →

Decision Tree

1) Heater stable?
If heater alarm or temperature fluctuating → go to Heater Error page. Fix heater first.
2) Reference air path OK?
Check reference air filter, tubing, fittings. Any blockage/leak causes drift. Ensure clean/dry air.
3) Probe tip / filter fouled?
Inspect for dust/ash buildup, plugged cap. Clean/replace. Fouling causes slow response + drifting.
4) Process conditions changed?
Confirm combustion/process is stable. Compare with other indicators (stack O₂, fuel/air ratio, draft changes).
5) Calibration line leakage?
A leaking cal valve/manifold can bleed air/gas and cause drift. Isolate and leak test.
6) Electrical grounding / noise?
Check cable shielding, grounding, loose terminals, water ingress in junction box. Noise can appear as unstable reading.
7) Still drifting after checks?
Suspect sensor aging/damage. Plan cell/assembly replacement and verify after replacement.

Fast Actions

  • Verify reference air filter and line integrity
  • Clean/inspect probe cap and sensor area
  • Confirm heater temperature stable
  • Check cal valve manifold for leakage
  • Check terminals/grounding and moisture ingress
