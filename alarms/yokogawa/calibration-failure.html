Alarm: Calibration Failure

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Analyzer: ZR22G Zirconia Oxygen Analyzer

Alarm Description

The Calibration Failure alarm is generated when the analyzer does not reach the expected oxygen value during zero or span calibration. When this alarm is active, calibration results are invalid and measurement accuracy cannot be guaranteed.

When This Alarm Typically Occurs

  • During automatic or manual calibration
  • After long operation without calibration
  • After maintenance on calibration gas lines

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Calibration gas not reaching the sensor
  2. Calibration solenoid valve not operating
  3. Incorrect, expired, or wrong concentration calibration gas
  4. Leak or blockage in calibration tubing
  5. Slow or degraded zirconia sensor response

Field Checks (Step-by-Step)

  • Confirm calibration gas pressure and flow at the analyzer inlet
  • Manually activate calibration valves from the analyzer menu
  • Check tubing for leaks, cracks, or blockage
  • Verify calibration gas concentration and expiry date
  • Observe oxygen response time during calibration

Corrective Actions

  • Restore calibration gas supply and correct pressure
  • Replace faulty calibration solenoid valve
  • Use correct certified zero/span gas
  • Repair or replace damaged tubing
  • Replace sensor probe if response is slow or unstable

Impact on Measurement

If calibration failure is ignored, oxygen readings may drift and combustion control or emissions reporting will be unreliable. Calibration must be successful before returning the analyzer to service.
