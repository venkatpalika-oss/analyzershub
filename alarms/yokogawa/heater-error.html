Alarm: Heater Error

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Applies To: ZR22G, other zirconia O₂ analyzers

Alarm Description

The Heater Error alarm indicates that the zirconia sensor heater is not operating within the required temperature range. Accurate oxygen measurement is not possible without proper heater operation.

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Heater open circuit or burnout
  2. Heater power supply failure
  3. Loose or damaged heater wiring
  4. Excessive process temperature
  5. Electronics board fault

Field Checks

  • Measure heater resistance and compare with specifications
  • Verify heater voltage at analyzer terminals
  • Inspect wiring connectors for looseness or corrosion
  • Check flue temperature at probe location

Corrective Actions

  • Replace heater assembly if resistance is abnormal
  • Restore or stabilize power supply
  • Repair or replace damaged wiring
  • Relocate probe if temperature exceeds limits
  • Replace electronics module if required

Impact on Measurement

When a heater error is active, oxygen readings are unreliable and should not be used for combustion control or reporting.
