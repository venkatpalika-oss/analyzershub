Alarm: Heater Error

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Analyzer: ZR22G Zirconia Oxygen Analyzer

Alarm Description

The Heater Error alarm indicates that the zirconia sensor heater is not operating within the required temperature range. Accurate oxygen measurement is not possible unless the sensor heater is functioning correctly.

Typical Symptoms

  • Oxygen reading frozen, zero, or invalid
  • Analyzer stuck in warm-up state
  • Repeated heater-related alarms after power-up
  • Sudden signal loss following process temperature increase

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Heater element open circuit or burnout
  2. Heater power supply failure
  3. Loose, damaged, or corroded heater wiring
  4. Excessive process or flue gas temperature
  5. Electronics module malfunction

Field Checks (Recommended Order)

  • Verify analyzer power supply and fuses
  • Check heater LED/status indication on the converter
  • Measure heater resistance and compare with specifications
  • Verify heater voltage at probe terminals
  • Inspect wiring and connectors for looseness or corrosion
  • Confirm process temperature is within probe limits

Corrective Actions

  • Replace heater assembly if resistance is abnormal
  • Restore or stabilize heater power supply
  • Repair or replace damaged heater wiring
  • Relocate probe or install heat shield if temperature is excessive
  • Replace electronics module if heater control fails

Impact on Measurement

When a heater error is active, oxygen measurement is unreliable and must not be used for combustion control or emissions reporting. The alarm must be cleared before returning the analyzer to service.
