Alarm: No Oxygen Signal

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Analyzer: ZR22G Zirconia Oxygen Analyzer

Alarm Description

The No Oxygen Signal alarm indicates that the analyzer is not receiving a valid oxygen measurement signal from the zirconia sensor. The oxygen output may be frozen, zero, or invalid.

Typical Symptoms

  • Oxygen value fixed at 0%, 21%, or last known value
  • Analyzer shows sensor error or invalid reading
  • Calibration cannot be performed
  • Signal drops suddenly after maintenance or shutdown

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Heater not operating or analyzer not fully warmed up
  2. Reference air supply blocked or missing
  3. Zirconia sensor probe failure
  4. Signal cable or terminal wiring fault
  5. Converter electronics failure

Field Checks (Recommended Order)

  • Verify heater status and confirm stable operating temperature
  • Check reference air flow and filter condition
  • Inspect probe for physical damage or excessive contamination
  • Measure sensor output signal at converter terminals
  • Check continuity of probe-to-converter wiring

Corrective Actions

  • Allow full warm-up or restore heater operation
  • Restore reference air supply and clean filters
  • Replace sensor probe if no output is present
  • Repair or replace damaged signal wiring
  • Replace converter electronics if required

Impact on Measurement

With no valid oxygen signal, analyzer output must not be used for combustion control, emissions monitoring, or reporting. The analyzer should be returned to normal operation before use.
