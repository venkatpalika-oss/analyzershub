Alarm: No Oxygen Signal

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Alarm Description

The No Oxygen Signal alarm indicates that the analyzer is not receiving a valid measurement signal from the zirconia sensor. The output may be fixed, zero, or marked invalid.

Typical Symptoms

  • Oxygen value fixed at 0%, 21%, or last known value
  • Sensor or signal error displayed
  • Calibration cannot be performed
  • Signal loss after shutdown or maintenance

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Heater not operating or analyzer not fully warmed up
  2. Reference air supply blocked or missing
  3. Zirconia sensor probe failure
  4. Signal cable or terminal wiring fault
  5. Converter electronics failure

Field Checks

  • Verify heater operation and sensor temperature
  • Check reference air flow and filters
  • Inspect probe condition and contamination
  • Measure sensor output at converter terminals
  • Check wiring continuity between probe and converter

Corrective Actions

  • Allow full warm-up or restore heater operation
  • Restore reference air supply
  • Replace zirconia probe if no output is present
  • Repair or replace damaged signal wiring
  • Replace converter electronics if required

Impact on Measurement

Without a valid oxygen signal, analyzer output must not be used for combustion control or emissions monitoring. Normal operation must be restored before relying on analyzer data.

Applicable Analyzers

  • Yokogawa ZR22G
  • Yokogawa OX400
  • Yokogawa TDLS8000
