Alarm: High Drift / Unstable Oxygen Reading

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Analyzer: ZR22G Zirconia Oxygen Analyzer

Alarm Description

The High Drift alarm indicates unstable or continuously drifting oxygen readings that do not correlate with actual process conditions. This alarm typically develops gradually and worsens over time if not corrected.

Typical Symptoms

  • Oxygen value slowly increases or decreases without process change
  • Large deviation between process oxygen and calibration gas value
  • Repeated calibration required to maintain accuracy
  • Reading fluctuates even at stable load conditions

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Sensor probe contamination (dust, ash, corrosion)
  2. Reference air leakage or blockage
  3. Process temperature fluctuations at probe location
  4. Electrical noise, poor grounding, or shielding issues
  5. Aging or end-of-life zirconia sensor

Field Checks (Recommended Order)

  • Inspect probe tip for ash, soot, or corrosion buildup
  • Check reference air tubing for leaks, kinks, or blockage
  • Verify process temperature stability at the probe location
  • Inspect grounding, shielding, and cable routing
  • Review calibration history and drift trend

Corrective Actions

  • Clean probe tip or replace sensor if contaminated
  • Repair or replace damaged reference air tubing
  • Improve probe mounting location if temperature fluctuates
  • Correct earthing and remove electrical interference sources
  • Replace zirconia sensor if drift persists after maintenance

Impact on Measurement

High drift directly affects combustion efficiency, emissions compliance, and process control. Ignoring this alarm can result in fuel wastage, increased emissions, and unreliable analyzer data.
