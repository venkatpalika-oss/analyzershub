Siemens MAXUM Edition II — Alarm Codes (Quick Actions)

Practical “first actions” for common MAXUM alarm situations. Use decision trees for full isolation.

← Back to MAXUM II Decision Trees →
Field tip: If multiple alarms appear together, start with carrier + sample conditioning. Fix pressure/flow stability before calibration or detector replacement.

Carrier / Pressure / Flow

  • Carrier Pressure Low/High — confirm supply, regulator stability, leaks, filter restriction
  • Flow Controller Fault — check controller status, setpoints, valve response, sensor wiring
  • Leak Suspected — soap test fittings, verify column/valve block connections, check vent lines

First action: verify pressure is stable (no hunting). Unstable pressure causes retention time shift + noisy baseline.

Oven / Temperature

  • Oven Not Ready — heater output, temp sensor, control loop, fan/airflow (if present)
  • Temperature Deviation — check setpoint vs actual, sensor drift, wiring, controller tuning

First action: confirm oven temperature is stable at setpoint before judging chromatogram quality.

Detector / Signal Quality

  • Baseline Noise/Drift — carrier purity, leaks, detector contamination, grounding
  • Detector Fault — electronics, sensor, power supplies, module seating
  • Signal Out of Range — incorrect gain settings, detector issue, wiring problem

Method / Results

  • Retention Time Shift — carrier flow change, column/oven temp drift, pressure instability
  • Poor Repeatability — injection valve wear, sample pressure instability, contamination
  • Missing Peaks — stream selection, valve timing, column degradation, sample issue

FID Specific (if installed)

  • FID Flameout — fuel/air pressures, ignition, dirty jet, flow imbalance
  • FID Ignition Fail — igniter issue, gas supply problem, wet/contaminated lines
