Alarm: No Signal / Signal Lost

Manufacturer: ABB
Applicable Analyzers: AZ10, AZ20, AZ25, AZ30, AZ40

Important: A “No Signal” alarm means the analyzer cannot produce a valid measurement. Analyzer output must not be used for control or reporting until resolved.

Alarm Description

The No Signal alarm indicates that the analyzer is not receiving a valid measurement signal from the sensor or optical path. This may occur suddenly or after maintenance, shutdowns, or process changes.

Typical Symptoms

  • Measured value fixed at zero or last known value
  • Analyzer reports invalid or bad quality status
  • Calibration cannot be initiated or fails immediately
  • Signal loss after power interruption or maintenance

Probable Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Sensor heater not operating or analyzer not fully warmed up
  2. Sensor probe failure or disconnection
  3. Reference air or purge path blocked (zirconia / TDLAS)
  4. Optical path obstruction or beam loss (TDLAS analyzers)
  5. Signal cable or terminal wiring fault
  6. Electronics or I/O module failure

Field Checks (Recommended Order)

  • Confirm analyzer power supply and internal status LEDs
  • Verify heater status and operating temperature
  • Inspect probe, optics, and mounting condition
  • Check reference air / purge flow and filters
  • Inspect signal wiring, connectors, and grounding
  • Review alarm history and event logs

Corrective Actions

  • Allow full warm-up or restore heater operation
  • Clean or replace sensor probe or optical windows
  • Restore reference air or purge supply
  • Repair or replace damaged wiring or connectors
  • Replace sensor or electronics module if no signal is detected

Impact on Measurement

When the No Signal alarm is active, analyzer data is invalid. Continued operation without resolving this alarm may lead to poor combustion control, emissions non-compliance, or unsafe operation.
