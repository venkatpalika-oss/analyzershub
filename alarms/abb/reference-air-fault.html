Reference Air Fault — ABB AZ10

This alarm indicates that the analyzer reference air supply is missing, restricted, or contaminated. Stable reference air is essential for accurate zirconia oxygen measurement.

Impact: Oxygen measurement may drift or become unstable while this alarm is active.

Probable Causes

  • Reference air filter blocked or saturated
  • Loss of reference air supply pressure
  • Purge or reference solenoid valve not operating
  • Leaks, kinks, or broken reference air tubing
  • Moisture or dust ingress into reference path

Field Checks

  • Verify reference air supply pressure and flow
  • Inspect and replace reference air filters if required
  • Check purge / reference solenoid valve operation
  • Inspect tubing for leaks, blockage, or damage
  • Confirm reference air exhaust flow is present

Corrective Actions

  • Restore clean, dry reference air supply
  • Replace blocked or contaminated filters
  • Repair or replace damaged tubing or fittings
  • Replace faulty purge / reference solenoid valves
Technician note: Many reference air faults are caused by moisture ingress or neglected filters. Regular inspection prevents repeat alarms.
