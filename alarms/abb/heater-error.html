Heater Fault — ABB AZ10

This alarm indicates that the zirconia sensor heater is unable to maintain the required operating temperature. Oxygen measurement is unreliable while this alarm is active.

Impact: Analyzer output is unstable or invalid when the heater is not operating correctly.

Alarm Description

The AZ10 continuously monitors heater current, voltage, and temperature feedback. A heater fault is triggered when the heater cannot reach or maintain its operating setpoint.

Probable Causes

  • Heater element open circuit or burnout
  • Loss of heater power supply or blown fuse
  • Loose, damaged, or corroded heater wiring
  • Excessive process temperature at the probe
  • Heater control electronics failure

Field Checks

  • Verify heater warm-up and status indication
  • Measure heater resistance and compare with specification
  • Check heater voltage at probe terminals
  • Inspect wiring, connectors, and cable glands
  • Confirm process temperature is within probe limits

Corrective Actions

  • Replace heater element or probe assembly
  • Restore heater power supply and replace fuses
  • Repair or replace damaged wiring
  • Install heat shielding or relocate probe if required
Technician note: Many heater alarms are secondary effects of voltage instability, moisture ingress, or excessive ambient heat — verify these before replacing electronics.
