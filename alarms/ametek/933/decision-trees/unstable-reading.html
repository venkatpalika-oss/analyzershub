Decision Tree: Unstable O₂ Reading

Symptom: reading fluctuates, noisy, unstable trend.

← Back to Trees ← Alarm Codes AMETEK 933 Main →
Rule: Do not calibrate until reading stabilizes.

Isolation steps

1) Check sample flow and pressure stability. Confirm there is no regulator hunting.
2) Check for leaks / air ingress on sample lines, fittings, and manifold. Tighten and re-check.
3) Check moisture/condensation:
  • Drain traps/knockout pots if fitted
  • Confirm heat tracing is on (if required)
  • Replace wet filters
4) Verify calibration gas stability (if on cal): correct cylinder, stable regulator, no leaks in cal manifold.
5) Electrical check:
  • Check sensor wiring terminals for looseness
  • Verify proper grounding/earthing
  • Inspect for water ingress in junction boxes
6) If still unstable after sample & wiring checks:
  • Suspect sensor aging or contamination
  • Check sensor temperature stability (if heated)
Field tip: If the reading becomes stable on calibration gas but unstable on process sample, the issue is likely in the sample conditioning (process moisture/pressure swings).
