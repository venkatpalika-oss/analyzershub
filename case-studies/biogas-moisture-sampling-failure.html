Biogas Analyzer Failure Due to Moisture & Sampling Design

Industry: Renewable Energy   |   Plant type: CBG Plant   |   Analyzer: Biogas Composition Analyzer

Background

A compressed biogas (CBG) plant experienced repeated failures of its online biogas composition analyzer installed upstream of gas engines.

Observed Problem

  • Analyzer showed unstable methane readings
  • Frequent sensor replacement was required
  • Analyzer worked normally during calibration

Initial (Wrong) Assumptions

  • Analyzer electronics were suspected
  • Sensor quality was blamed
  • Calibration frequency was increased

Actual Root Cause

The sampling line was routed with a downward slope toward the analyzer, causing condensate to flow directly into filters and sensors during night-time cooling.

Corrective Actions

1) Re-routed sample line with proper slope
2) Installed knock-out pot and drain
3) Added dew point monitoring

Result After Fix

  • Analyzer stabilized within 24 hours
  • No sensor failures for 9+ months
  • Reduced maintenance workload

Lessons Learned

  • Sampling design is more critical than analyzer selection
  • Moisture must be assumed in biogas systems
  • Calibration cannot fix wet sampling systems
