Why CEMS Exists – Origin and Significance

Environmental compliance did not begin with automation. It began with pollution, public concern, and regulation.

Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems (CEMS) were introduced because periodic testing was not enough to ensure compliance or protect the environment.

If emissions are not measured continuously, violations go unnoticed.
