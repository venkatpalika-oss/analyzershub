Analyzer Reliability vs Accuracy – What Really Matters?

In analyzer datasheets, accuracy gets the spotlight. In real plants, reliability decides everything.

Engineers often debate analyzer accuracy in decimals and percentages. Technicians face a different reality: an analyzer that is perfectly accurate but frequently unavailable is practically useless.

An accurate analyzer that is down gives zero value. A reliable analyzer gives usable information every day.

What Is Accuracy?

Accuracy describes how close a measurement is to the true value, usually defined under ideal laboratory conditions.

  • Clean samples
  • Stable temperature
  • Controlled pressure
  • Perfect calibration gases

What Is Reliability?

  • Staying online
  • Providing consistent trends
  • Recovering quickly from disturbances
  • Maintaining long-term stability
Reliability means operators trust the number they see.

Why Reliability Matters More

1. Control Needs Trends

Control systems react to trends, not laboratory perfection.

2. Alarms Require Availability

An analyzer in maintenance mode cannot protect people or equipment.

3. Compliance Is Continuous

Regulators value data availability as much as accuracy.

Most analyzer failures are caused by sample systems — not analyzers.

Conclusion

Accuracy impresses on paper. Reliability delivers in the field.
