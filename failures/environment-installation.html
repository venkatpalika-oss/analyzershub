Shelter & Environment Failures

Analyzers are precision instruments, but they are often installed in harsh industrial environments without adequate protection. Poor shelters silently shorten analyzer life and reliability.

Field reality: If the shelter is wrong, even the best analyzer will fail.

Typical Symptoms

  • Random analyzer resets or reboots
  • Frequent electronic board failures
  • Sensor drift without process change
  • Seasonal failures (summer / monsoon / winter)
  • High maintenance frequency with no clear cause

Common Environmental Causes

  • High ambient temperature exceeding analyzer limits
  • High humidity and condensation inside enclosures
  • Dust ingress due to poor sealing
  • Vibration from nearby rotating equipment
  • Corrosive atmosphere (H₂S, acid fumes, ammonia)
  • Unstable or poor-quality power supply

Frequent Shelter Failure Modes

  • Overheating: electronics drift, sensors age faster
  • Condensation: short circuits and corrosion
  • Dust loading: fans clog, cooling fails
  • Vibration: loose connections, cracked solder joints
  • Corrosion: terminal and connector degradation

Correct Troubleshooting Order

1) Measure actual shelter temperature & humidity
2) Inspect enclosure sealing, gaskets, and filters
3) Verify shelter ventilation / AC operation
4) Check grounding and power quality
5) Inspect for vibration and mechanical stress
6) Only then troubleshoot analyzer electronics

Common Design & Installation Mistakes

  • Using non-air-conditioned shelters in hot climates
  • No humidity control or heaters
  • Improper cable gland sealing
  • Installing analyzers near vibration sources
  • Ignoring hazardous area and corrosion class

Best Practices

  • Design shelter based on worst-case environment
  • Maintain positive pressure and clean air
  • Monitor temperature and humidity continuously
  • Isolate analyzers from vibration
  • Treat the shelter as part of the analyzer system
