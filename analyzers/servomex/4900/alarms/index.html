Servomex 4900 — Alarm Decision Trees

This page provides technician-focused alarm decision trees for the Servomex 4900 multigas analyzer. Follow the sequence strictly to avoid unnecessary sensor replacement or recalibration.

← Back to Analyzer Servomex Analyzers →
Technician rule: Never acknowledge alarms blindly. Flow stability and sample condition must be verified before calibration or sensor replacement.

Sample Flow Alarm

Indicates insufficient or unstable sample flow reaching the analyzer.

  • Check 1: Sample source pressure available?
  • Check 2: Filters blocked or wet?
  • Check 3: Regulator hunting or unstable?
  • Check 4: Sample pump (if used) running?
Do not: Calibrate with unstable flow. This will create false drift and repeat alarms.

Oxygen Sensor Fault

Paramagnetic oxygen sensor signal is outside acceptable limits.

  • Check 1: Sample flow stable and within specification?
  • Check 2: Zero gas truly oxygen-free?
  • Check 3: Moisture contamination present?
  • Check 4: Sensor ageing or mechanical damage?
Sensor replacement should be the last step, not the first reaction.

IR Channel Fault (CO / CO₂)

Infrared signal is weak, unstable, or outside calibrated range.

  • Check 1: Sample pressure within design limits?
  • Check 2: Optical cell contamination?
  • Check 3: Calibration gas concentration correct?
  • Check 4: Electronics temperature stable?
IR channels are sensitive to pressure and temperature changes. Stabilize process before recalibration.

Communication Alarm

Analyzer data not updating in DCS or SCADA.

  • Check 1: Analyzer powered and running?
  • Check 2: Ethernet cable / switch status?
  • Check 3: IP address and subnet correct?
  • Check 4: DCS mapping, scaling, and quality bits?
Many “analyzer faults” are actually DCS mapping or quality bit issues.
