CBG / Bio-CNG Plant Analyzers

Analyzer systems required to upgrade raw biogas into Compressed Biogas (CBG) / Bio-CNG meeting fuel quality, safety, and regulatory requirements.

Technician rule: If CBG purity or pressure trips occur, check CO₂ removal, moisture dryness, and oxygen ingress before blaming compressors or dispensers.

Gas Quality Analyzers (Mandatory)

  • Methane (CH₄): fuel quality and calorific value
  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂): upgrading efficiency
  • Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S): corrosion control
  • Oxygen (O₂): safety and explosion prevention

Moisture / Dew Point Analyzers

  • Dew point monitoring before compressors
  • Protection of storage cascades and dispensers
  • Compliance with pipeline / fuel dryness limits

CO₂ Removal Performance Monitoring

  • CO₂ analyzer before and after upgrading skid
  • Membrane / PSA performance trending
  • Early detection of membrane failure

Compression & Storage Safety

  • Fixed gas detectors (CH₄, H₂S)
  • Oxygen deficiency monitoring in enclosures
  • Pressure and temperature interlocks

Typical Analyzer Locations

  • After biogas upgrading skid
  • Before high-pressure compressors
  • Before cascade storage
  • At dispensing stations

Common CBG Plant Issues

  • High CO₂ due to membrane leakage
  • Moisture carryover causing compressor trips
  • Oxygen ingress from leaky digesters
  • False trips due to poor analyzer conditioning
