Engine / CHP Exhaust Gas Analyzers — Biogas Plants

Exhaust gas analyzers used on biogas engines and CHP units to control combustion efficiency, protect engines, and meet emission compliance requirements.

Technician rule: If NOx or CO suddenly increases, check air–fuel ratio, ignition timing, and methane quality before suspecting exhaust analyzer drift.

Why Exhaust Gas Monitoring Is Required

  • Optimizes combustion efficiency
  • Protects engines from overheating and knock
  • Ensures compliance with emission norms
  • Detects misfiring and poor gas quality

Key Exhaust Parameters Measured

  • O₂: excess air / combustion balance
  • CO: incomplete combustion indicator
  • NOx: combustion temperature & emissions
  • CO₂: combustion efficiency trending

Measurement Technologies

  • Electrochemical sensors (O₂, CO, NOx)
  • NDIR (CO, CO₂)
  • Zirconia oxygen sensors

Typical Installation Locations

  • Engine exhaust manifold
  • After turbocharger
  • Before or after exhaust silencer
  • Before SCR / oxidation catalyst

Common Engine / CHP Issues

  • High CO due to rich combustion
  • High NOx due to lean mixture or timing
  • Sensor poisoning from sulfur compounds
  • Sampling probe clogging with soot

Maintenance & Best Practices

  • Regular probe and filter cleaning
  • Calibration using certified exhaust gases
  • Verify sample line heating to avoid condensation
  • Cross-check analyzer with portable instruments
