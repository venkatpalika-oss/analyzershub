Biogas Composition Analyzer

A biogas composition analyzer continuously measures the major constituents of biogas to ensure safe operation, energy efficiency, and equipment protection in biogas plants.

What Does a Biogas Composition Analyzer Measure?

  • Methane (CH₄) – Indicates energy content and calorific value
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂) – Reduces heating value of biogas
  • Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S) – Toxic and highly corrosive
  • Oxygen (O₂) – Detects air ingress and explosion risk

Typical Analyzer Technologies Used

Gas Component Measurement Technology
CH₄ / CO₂ NDIR (Non-Dispersive Infrared)
H₂S Electrochemical / UV-Fluorescence
O₂ Electrochemical / Paramagnetic

Where Is the Analyzer Installed?

  • Raw biogas line after digester
  • Before and after H₂S scrubber
  • Upstream of gas engines or boilers
  • Before biogas upgrading (CBG / Bio-CNG)

Why Biogas Composition Measurement Is Critical

  • Protects gas engines from corrosion and knocking
  • Prevents explosive mixtures due to oxygen ingress
  • Optimizes H₂S removal systems
  • Improves plant efficiency and uptime
  • Supports compliance with safety standards

Common Problems Seen in Biogas Plants

  • High H₂S damaging engines and compressors
  • Low methane causing power output loss
  • Oxygen ingress due to leaking digesters
  • Condensation affecting analyzer accuracy

Maintenance & Calibration Considerations

  • Regular calibration using certified span gases
  • Sample gas conditioning (moisture removal)
  • Sensor replacement interval for H₂S cells
  • Periodic leak checks in sampling system

Typical Users

  • Compressed Biogas (CBG) plants
  • Distillery spent wash biogas plants
  • Sewage treatment plants (STP)
  • Agricultural and food waste digesters
