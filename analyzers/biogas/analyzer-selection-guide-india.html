Biogas & CBG Analyzer Selection Guide — India

A practical, vendor-neutral guide to selecting the right analyzers for biogas and CBG plants in India, based on plant size, application, compliance needs, and real operating conditions.

Technician rule: Over-specification wastes money. Under-specification causes repeated failures. Select analyzers based on gas quality, moisture, H₂S load, and operating discipline.

Step 1: Identify Your Plant Type

  • Standalone biogas + engine / CHP
  • Biogas with flaring only
  • CBG / Bio-CNG upgrading plant
  • Industrial / distillery-based biogas

Plant type defines gas quality limits and analyzer criticality.

Step 2: Mandatory Gas Analyzers

  • CH₄ / CO₂: energy content & upgrading control
  • H₂S: corrosion & safety
  • O₂: air ingress & explosion prevention
  • Moisture / Dew Point: compressor & pipeline protection

These are non-negotiable for most Indian plants.

Step 3: Process-Side Analyzers

  • pH (online or lab)
  • VFA (online or periodic)
  • Alkalinity
  • Ammonia (high-protein feedstocks)

Strongly recommended for stable, long-term operation.

Step 4: Safety & Compliance

  • Fixed CH₄ gas detectors (LEL)
  • H₂S gas detectors in digester & engine rooms
  • Oxygen deficiency monitors (enclosures)
  • Exhaust gas analyzers (CHP engines)

Required for PESO, OMC, and CPCB inspections.

Step 5: Technology Selection Tips

  • NDIR for CH₄ / CO₂ (robust, low maintenance)
  • Electrochemical for H₂S & O₂ (cost-effective)
  • Chilled mirror for critical dew point
  • Avoid lab-grade analyzers in harsh field locations
