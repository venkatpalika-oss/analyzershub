H₂S Analyzer & Gas Detectors for Biogas Plants

Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) is one of the most critical contaminants in biogas. Continuous H₂S monitoring is essential for engine protection, corrosion control, and personnel safety.

Why H₂S Monitoring Is Mandatory in Biogas Plants

  • Highly toxic to humans even at low ppm levels
  • Severely corrosive to engines, compressors and pipelines
  • Reduces engine oil life and causes premature failures
  • Affects performance of biogas upgrading systems

What Does an H₂S Analyzer Measure?

  • Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S) concentration in ppm or %
  • Trend monitoring for scrubber efficiency
  • Alarm generation for high H₂S levels

Types of H₂S Measurement in Biogas Plants

Type Application
Online H₂S Analyzer Continuous measurement in biogas pipelines
Fixed H₂S Gas Detector Area safety monitoring (engine room, digester)
Portable H₂S Gas Detector Maintenance, leak checks, confined space entry

Common Measurement Technologies

  • Electrochemical sensors (most common)
  • UV fluorescence (high accuracy, higher cost)
  • Colorimetric tubes (spot checks only)

Typical Installation Locations

  • Raw biogas line after digester
  • Before and after H₂S scrubbers
  • Engine room and compressor room (area detectors)
  • Gas storage and upgrading skids

Common Problems Seen in Plants

  • Frequent sensor failure due to high moisture
  • Delayed alarms due to poor sample conditioning
  • Scrubber saturation not detected early
  • Ignoring calibration intervals

Maintenance & Calibration Considerations

  • Regular calibration using certified H₂S span gas
  • Sensor replacement as per manufacturer interval
  • Proper moisture removal in sampling systems
  • Periodic bump test for safety detectors

Typical Users

  • Compressed Biogas (CBG) plants
  • Distillery and spent wash biogas plants
  • Sewage treatment plants (STP)
  • Food waste and agricultural digesters
