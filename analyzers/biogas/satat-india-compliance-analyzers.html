SATAT / India Compliance Analyzers — Biogas & CBG Plants

Analyzer systems required to meet SATAT, OMC, PNGRB, PESO and CPCB expectations for Compressed Biogas (CBG) and biogas plants in India.

Technician rule: Most SATAT non-compliance issues are caused by missing analyzers, poor sample conditioning, or lack of calibration records — not plant capacity.

Gas Quality Compliance (CBG Output)

  • Methane (CH₄): fuel quality & CV compliance
  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂): upgrading efficiency
  • Oxygen (O₂): explosion prevention
  • Hydrogen sulfide (H₂S): corrosion & safety

Required by OMCs (IOCL / BPCL / HPCL) before gas offtake.

Moisture / Dew Point Compliance

  • Dew point measurement before compressors
  • Protection of cascades and dispensers
  • Prevention of hydrate formation

Moisture failures are one of the most common SATAT rejection reasons.

Safety & PESO Requirements

  • Fixed CH₄ gas detectors (LEL)
  • H₂S gas detectors in digester & engine areas
  • Oxygen deficiency monitoring in enclosures

Mandatory for PESO approval and site audits.

Emission & Environmental Monitoring

  • Engine / CHP exhaust analyzers (O₂, CO, NOx)
  • Stack emission monitoring (where applicable)
  • Data logging for audits

Required by CPCB / SPCB for larger plants.

Process Monitoring Expectations

  • pH monitoring in digesters
  • VFA and alkalinity trending
  • Ammonia monitoring for toxicity control
