Yokogawa ZR22G Oxygen Analyzer

Manufacturer: Yokogawa

Analyzer Type: In-situ Zirconia Oxygen Analyzer

1. Overview

The Yokogawa ZR22G is an in-situ zirconia oxygen analyzer used for continuous oxygen measurement in combustion applications such as boilers, furnaces, heaters, and incinerators.

2. Measurement Principle

The analyzer operates on a zirconium oxide (ZrOâ‚‚) sensor heated to a high temperature. The oxygen concentration is calculated from the EMF generated between the process gas and reference air.

4. Installation Notes

  • Install probe at correct insertion length
  • Avoid high vibration locations
  • Ensure clean, dry reference air
  • Provide proper grounding and shielding

6. Troubleshooting â€“ No Oxygen Reading

Possible Causes (Most Common First)

  1. Heater not operating or under-temperature
  2. Reference air supply blocked or leaking
  3. Zirconia sensor end-of-life
  4. Signal cable or terminal issue
  5. Converter electronics failure

Field Checks

  • Verify heater current and heater status
  • Check reference air flow at analyzer inlet
  • Inspect probe tip for cracks or deposits
  • Measure output signal at terminals

Corrective Actions

  • Restore heater power or replace heater
  • Clean or replace reference air filter
  • Replace sensor probe if degraded
  • Repair wiring or replace electronics

7. Troubleshooting â€“ Heater Alarm / Heater Error

Possible Causes

  1. Heater open or short circuit
  2. Power supply instability
  3. Excessive flue temperature
  4. Electronics board failure

Field Checks

  • Measure heater resistance
  • Check heater voltage at terminals
  • Inspect probe for overheating damage
  • Verify grounding and earthing

Corrective Actions

  • Replace heater assembly
  • Stabilize power supply
  • Correct installation location
  • Replace converter electronics if required

8. Troubleshooting â€“ High Drift / Unstable Reading

Possible Causes

  1. Sensor contamination due to dust or ash
  2. Reference air leakage
  3. Process temperature fluctuations
  4. Electrical noise or poor grounding

Field Checks

  • Inspect probe tip for deposits
  • Check reference air tubing and fittings
  • Observe process conditions
  • Verify grounding continuity

Corrective Actions

  • Clean or replace probe
  • Fix reference air leaks
  • Improve probe shielding or location
  • Correct grounding and cable routing

9. Troubleshooting â€“ Calibration Failure

Possible Causes

  1. Calibration gas not reaching sensor
  2. Solenoid valve malfunction
  3. Incorrect gas concentration
  4. Slow or degraded sensor response

Field Checks

  • Verify calibration gas pressure and flow
  • Manually actuate calibration valves
  • Check calibration gas certificate
  • Observe response time

Corrective Actions

  • Restore gas supply or tubing
  • Replace faulty solenoid valve
  • Use correct certified calibration gas
  • Replace sensor if response is slow

10. Calibration Guidelines

  • Allow full analyzer warm-up
  • Use certified span gas
  • Confirm valve operation before calibration
  • Allow sufficient stabilization time

11. Preventive Maintenance

  • Inspect reference air filter monthly
  • Check heater current during PM
  • Calibrate as per site SOP
  • Inspect probe for buildup or corrosion

12. Field Tips

  • Never assess sensor health before warm-up completes
  • Most false readings are reference air related
  • Sudden drift usually indicates contamination
  • Always check heater status first during faults
