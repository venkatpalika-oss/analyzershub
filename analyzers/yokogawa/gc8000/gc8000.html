1) Startup & Health Checks

A — Visual checks
  • Cabinet clean/dry, no loose tubing, no gas smell.
  • Confirm utilities: power stable, instrument air (if used), carrier gas, sample supply.
  • Check sample conditioning: filters/traps/drains, heaters/heat tracing.
B — Status & timestamps
  • Confirm analyzer mode is RUN and schedule is active.
  • Confirm last analysis timestamp is updating (based on cycle time).
  • Check active alarms and record text/time.
Healthy signs: stable carrier/sample pressures, stable oven temperature, repeatable peaks, no critical alarms.

2) No Peaks / No Results

Symptoms
  • No chromatogram peaks (flat line) or extremely weak peaks.
  • Results not generated / analysis fails / sequence stops.
Quick isolate
  • No peaks usually means: sample not reaching / injection not happening / carrier issue.
  • Peaks present but no result usually means: configuration/calculation issue or comms mapping.
Most common causes
  • Carrier low / regulator creep / leaks
  • Sample line blocked / filter plugged / liquid carryover
  • Valve not switching due to instrument air or solenoid fault
Use alarm trees for faster isolation: Carrier · Valves · Oven

3) Unstable / Poor Repeatability

Symptoms
  • Component values fluctuate abnormally between runs.
  • Retention time shift, peak area variation, unstable baseline.
Top causes
  • Sample conditioning problem (water/heavy HC contamination)
  • Unstable carrier pressure or micro-leaks
  • Oven temperature instability
  • Valve switching inconsistency
Actions
  • Drain traps, replace filters, verify sample regulator stability.
  • Leak test critical fittings and valve manifold.
  • Confirm oven holds steady for 15–20 minutes before comparing runs.

4) Drift / Calibration Issues

Diagnosis
  • Slow drift → contamination, regulator creep, aging components.
  • Sudden shift → leak, method/config change, valve failure, sample upset.
Calibration checks
  • Verify calibration gas cylinder validity and correct connection.
  • Confirm calibration line is leak-free and switching works correctly.
  • Run repeat calibration/verification and compare trend.
If calibration fails repeatedly: suspect leaks, wrong gas, stuck valve, or contaminated sample system.

5) Sample System Troubleshooting

Symptoms
  • No peaks / weak peaks / slow response to process changes.
  • Frequent filter plugging or liquid accumulation.
Checks
  • Replace/clean sample filters and coalescers.
  • Drain liquid traps; check heat tracing (if required).
  • Check sample regulator and restrictor for blockage.
  • Confirm sample pressure is available from process.

6) Carrier / Pressure / Flow

Checks
  • Carrier cylinder pressure and correct gas type.
  • Regulator outlet stable (no hunting).
  • Leak test carrier path and fittings.
If stable carrier returns, peaks and retention stabilize automatically.

7) Oven / Temperature

Checks
  • Setpoint vs actual temperature stability.
  • Heater power/fuses and sensor wiring.
  • Ventilation and cabinet cooling airflow.

8) Valve / Sequence

Checks
  • Instrument air pressure/quality (water/oil causes sticking).
  • Solenoid operation (command vs actual).
  • Valve manifold contamination or internal leakage.

9) DCS / Communications

Common causes
  • Wrong IP/subnet/gateway or duplicate IP
  • Mapping/register mismatch in DCS
  • Update rate mismatch (GC updates per cycle)
Use the comms tree for step-by-step isolation: Communication / DCS Decision Tree

10) Preventive Maintenance

  • Weekly: drain traps, check filters, verify pressures stable.
  • Monthly: leak check critical fittings, review trend repeatability.
  • Quarterly: service sample conditioning, verify valve switching, confirm oven stability.
  • Planned: replace aging regulators/solenoids based on performance and site strategy.

11) Escalation Checklist (Before Vendor Support)

Collect these details
  • Model + serial, module details
  • Alarm list (text + timestamps) + screenshots
  • Last good run time + last calibration time
  • Carrier pressure, sample pressure/flow, oven temperature (actual vs setpoint)
  • What actions were done (filters changed, leak tested, restart, etc.)
