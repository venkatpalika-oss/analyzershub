Yokogawa GC8000 — Process Gas Chromatograph

Technician-ready troubleshooting: sampling, carrier stability, oven stability, valve sequencing, alarms and communication to DCS.

← Back to Search Alarm Decision Trees → Alarm Codes → Sampling System →
Best practice: If results look wrong, first stabilize carrier pressure and sampling conditioning. Many “GC problems” are actually sampling problems.

Common Symptoms

No peaks: carrier low / valve not switching / sample blocked.

Retention shift: oven instability or carrier hunting.

Baseline noise: contamination / detector / grounding.

Recommended Troubleshooting Order

1) Check alarms + last good analysis time
2) Check carrier stability (no hunting)
3) Check sample conditioning + filters
4) Confirm oven temperature stable
5) Confirm valve switching + method timing
