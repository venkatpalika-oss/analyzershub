GC8000 Sampling System Troubleshooting

Find the root cause of “no peaks”, slow response, drifting results, or frequent filter plugging.

Back to GC8000 Main → Alarms Index →
Safety: Sampling systems can contain hazardous gases. Follow isolation and depressurization procedure. Leak test after any disconnection.

Typical Sampling Path (Quick Reference)

Process Tap → Probe/Filter → Heat Trace (if needed) → Fast Loop → Pressure Regulator → Coalescer/Trap → Analyzer
Golden rule: If sample pressure/flow is unstable, GC results will be unstable even if the GC is healthy.

Fast Diagnosis: Choose Your Symptom

1) No Peaks / Very Weak Peaks

Chromatogram flat or tiny peaks.

2) Slow Response

Process changes take too long to reflect in results.

3) Drifting / Poor Repeatability

Values wander between runs without process reason.

4) Frequent Filter Plugging / Liquid Carryover

Filters block quickly; liquid found in traps.

1) No Peaks / Very Weak Peaks

Step A — Confirm sample supply exists
  • Check upstream process pressure at sampling point (if available)
  • Check isolation valves open on sample line
Step B — Check sample pressure regulator
  • Regulator outlet stable? Any hunting/creep?
  • Outlet pressure too low can cause no injection / weak peaks
Step C — Check filters/traps
  • Replace clogged filter element
  • Drain traps/coalescers (water/hydrocarbon)
Step D — Quick restriction test
  • Check for kinked tubing or blocked restrictor/orifice
  • Check fast loop flow (if installed)
If sample is OK but still no peaks, go to:
  • Carrier / Pressure Decision Tree
  • Valve / Sequence Decision Tree

2) Slow Response (Lagging Results)

Most common causes
  • Fast loop not flowing / insufficient flow rate
  • Long sample line without heat tracing (condensation)
  • Regulator restricting flow too much
Fix actions
  • Restore fast loop flow (check needle valve / DP)
  • Check heat tracing and insulation for heavy HC/wet gas
  • Confirm sample line slopes to drains (avoid liquid traps in line)

3) Drift / Poor Repeatability (Sampling Related)

Check for liquid carryover
  • Any liquid in traps? Water/condensate?
  • Any sudden weather effect (ambient temperature drop) causing condensation?
Check pressure stability
  • Sample regulator hunting causes peak area variation
  • Micro-leaks on sample fittings introduce air and affect composition
Verification: after fixing sampling issues, confirm 2–3 consecutive runs are stable.

4) Frequent Filter Plugging / Liquid Carryover

Likely causes
  • Dirty process (solids/aerosols) → need better pre-filter or cyclone
  • Condensation in line → add heat tracing or relocate conditioning closer to tap
  • Coalescer not sized/installed correctly → liquid bypass
If liquids reach the GC, it can cause long-term contamination and drift. Fix at sampling level quickly.
Fix actions
  • Improve probe filtration and use correct filter micron rating
  • Ensure heat tracing works and insulation is intact
  • Increase drainage frequency and check auto-drains (if installed)

Final Verification Checklist

  • Stable sample pressure/flow (no hunting)
  • No liquid accumulation after normal operation period
  • Peaks stable, retention consistent, repeatability improved
  • 2–3 consecutive results align with expected trend
