Siemens MAXUM Edition II — Process Gas Chromatograph

Troubleshooting reference for analyzer technicians: sampling, carrier stability, oven temperature stability, valve sequencing, alarms, and communication to DCS/PLC.

← Back to Search Alarm Decision Trees → Alarm Codes → Chromatogram Guide →
Best practice: For most GC issues, first stabilize carrier pressure and sampling conditioning. Many “GC faults” are actually sampling/utility problems.

Common Symptoms

No peaks: carrier low, valve not switching, sample blocked.

Retention time shift: oven instability or carrier hunting.

Repeatability poor: sampling contamination, pressure instability, or sticking valves.

Recommended Troubleshooting Order

1) Check alarms and last analysis time
2) Stabilize carrier (no hunting)
3) Check sample conditioning (filters, traps, drains)
4) Confirm oven stable at setpoint
5) Confirm valve switching / method timing
