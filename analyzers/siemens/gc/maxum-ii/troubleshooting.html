Siemens MAXUM Edition II — Troubleshooting Guide

Technician-focused troubleshooting for Siemens MAXUM process gas chromatographs: symptoms → checks → fixes → escalation.

Alarm Decision Trees → Back to Analyzer Search
Safety: Follow plant permit and isolation rules. Confirm power isolation before opening modules. Handle carrier and calibration gases using approved procedures.

1) Startup & Basic Health Checks

  • Inspect cabinet for leaks, condensation, loose tubing, abnormal heat or odor
  • Verify power, carrier gas, sample pressure, instrument air
  • Check analyzer status, alarms, method state, last run time

2) No Results / No Peaks

  • No sample reaching GC (filters, valves, low pressure)
  • Carrier gas missing or unstable
  • Injection/valve sequence not occurring
  • Detector not ready or not powered

3) Bad Peaks (Tailing / Broad / Shifted)

  • Column contamination or aging
  • Flow instability or leaks
  • Incorrect oven temperature or valve timing

4) Drift / Repeatability Issues

  • Unstable sample or carrier pressure
  • Detector baseline instability
  • Calibration gas or regulator creep

5) Sample System Problems

  • Blocked filters, liquid carryover
  • Heat tracing failures
  • Sample pump or regulator faults

6) Oven / Temperature Problems

  • Heater or temperature sensor failure
  • Door or gasket leakage
  • Unstable or slow temperature ramp

7) Detector Issues

  • No baseline or saturated signal
  • Contamination or wrong carrier flow
  • Detector module diagnostics active

8) DCS / Communication Issues

  • Bad quality or stale values at DCS
  • Protocol, network, or mapping errors

9) Preventive Maintenance

  • Weekly: filters, traps, pressure checks
  • Monthly: calibration review and drift trending
  • Quarterly: valve actuation inspection

10) Escalation Checklist

  • Analyzer model and serial numbers
  • Alarm text with timestamps
  • Method name and last calibration
  • Pressures, temperatures, and actions taken
