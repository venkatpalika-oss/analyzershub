Safety: Follow plant permits & isolation rules. Before opening enclosures, confirm power isolation where required. For carrier / calibration gases, check regulators, leak tests, and venting.

1) Startup & Basic Health Checks

Step A — Visual & Environment
  • Check analyzer cabinet: dust, water ingress, loose tubing, abnormal smell/heat.
  • Confirm utilities: power stable, instrument air (if used), carrier gas pressure, sample pressure/flow.
Step B — System Status
  • Check instrument status page: alarms, module state, method running, last run time.
  • Confirm time/date and method schedule (if runs are timed).
Step C — Quick Leak Check (Sampling side)
  • Confirm sample line not blocked. Check filters and condensate traps.
  • Confirm sample pressure regulator setpoint is stable (no hunting).

If any alarm exists: record alarm text, time, and affected module before making changes.

2) No Results / No Peaks

Likely causes
  • No sample reaching GC (blocked filter, closed valve, low pressure, pump failure).
  • Carrier gas issue (empty cylinder, regulator shut, low pressure).
  • Method not running / injection not happening / valve sequence not switching.
  • Detector not powered or not stable.
Checks
  • Check sample flow indicators (if fitted). Verify sample inlet valve positions.
  • Confirm carrier pressure at regulator and at analyzer inlet (if gauge available).
  • Check whether a run started: timestamps, “last injection”, “valve events”.
  • Check detector status: warm-up complete, baseline present, no critical alarm.
Fix actions
  • Replace/clean sample filter, drain traps, verify sample conditioning.
  • Restore carrier gas pressure; verify correct carrier for the method.
  • Restart method/run sequence (only after noting current configuration).
  • If detector shows “not ready”, allow stabilization; check power/module supply.

3) Bad Peaks (tailing, broad peaks, retention shift)

Likely causes
  • Column contamination, column aging, wrong oven temperature or ramp.
  • Leak in sample/carrier path causing flow instability.
  • Valve timing issues (valve not switching or leaking internally).
  • Wrong carrier flow / pressure.
Checks
  • Compare current retention times vs “known good” trend.
  • Verify oven temperature and method setpoints are correct.
  • Check for unstable pressure readings; listen for valve actuation.
  • Inspect traps/filters for contamination (oil, water, heavy hydrocarbons).
Fix actions
  • Stabilize sample conditioning; replace filters; confirm no liquid carryover.
  • Leak test fittings; tighten/replace ferrules where required.
  • Verify valve supply/actuation; repair/replace leaking valves if suspected.
  • Schedule column maintenance/replacement if peaks remain degraded.

4) Drift / Repeatability Issues

Likely causes
  • Unstable sample pressure/flow, temperature fluctuation, or carrier instability.
  • Detector baseline instability (contamination, power issues).
  • Calibration gas problems (wrong concentration, regulator creep, leaks).
Checks
  • Review trends: sample pressure/flow, carrier pressure, oven temperature.
  • Perform a short repeatability test (same stream, multiple runs).
  • Leak check calibration line and sample switching valve block.
Fix actions
  • Stabilize conditioning system: heat tracing, filters, traps, pressure regulators.
  • Check detector electronics grounding and stable power.
  • Replace suspect regulator; verify correct calibration gas cylinder.

5) Sample System Problems (pressure/flow)

Symptoms
  • Low sample pressure/flow alarms, intermittent peaks, slow response.
  • Condensation or liquid carryover in sample line.
Checks
  • Inspect sample filter, coalescer, traps, and drains.
  • Confirm heat tracing (if used) and sample line temperature.
  • Check for blocked restrictors or stuck regulators.
Fix actions
  • Replace filters; clean/replace restrictors; drain traps.
  • Restore heat tracing; ensure sample stays above dew point.
  • Verify sample pump operation (if present).

6) Oven / Temperature Problems

Likely causes
  • Oven heater failure, temperature sensor issue, controller fault.
  • Door/gasket leaks causing unstable oven temperature.
Checks
  • Compare actual vs setpoint temperature; look for oscillation or slow ramp.
  • Check any “oven not ready” or “heater fault” alarm text.
Fix actions
  • Verify heater supply; inspect heater circuit (as per plant electrical rules).
  • Check temperature sensor wiring; replace sensor if faulty.

7) Detector Issues

Symptoms
  • No baseline, saturated signal, noisy baseline, unstable zero.
Checks
  • Confirm detector power and warm-up status.
  • Check for contamination in sample path; verify correct carrier and flows.
  • Review detector module diagnostics (fault codes/alarms).
Fix actions
  • Stabilize flows; purge system if method allows.
  • Clean/replace contaminated parts as per OEM procedure.
  • If module fault persists, plan module swap or OEM support.

8) DCS/Network/Communication Issues

Common symptoms
  • DCS shows bad quality / stale values, or no update.
  • Analyzer runs locally but values don’t reach DCS.
Checks
  • Confirm network link: switch port LEDs, cable, IP settings.
  • Check if values update on local HMI/software but not DCS.
  • Verify protocol status (Modbus/OPC/etc.) and mapping.
Fix actions
  • Correct IP/subnet/gateway; confirm no duplicate IP.
  • Restart comms service (if allowed) and confirm tag mapping.
  • Escalate with snapshots: IP settings, protocol status, DCS point list.

9) Preventive Maintenance (Technician Routine)

  • Weekly: check sample filters/traps, confirm stable pressures and temperatures.
  • Monthly: verify calibration cycle, check for leaks, review drift trends.
  • Quarterly: inspect valve actuation, service sample conditioning (as applicable).
  • Planned: column performance review and replacement schedule (based on peaks/retention).

10) Escalation Checklist (Before Vendor Support)

  • Analyzer model & serial / module IDs
  • Alarm text + timestamp + screenshot/photo
  • Method name, last run time, last calibration time
  • Sample pressure/flow, carrier pressure, oven temperature (actual vs set)
  • What actions were taken (filters changed, leak test, restart, etc.)
