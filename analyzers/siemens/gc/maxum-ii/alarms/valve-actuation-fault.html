Valve Actuation / Sequence Fault — Decision Tree

Use this guide when valves are not switching, injection events are missing, timing is incorrect, sequence errors occur, or pneumatic supply issues are suspected.

Safety: Pneumatic lines may be pressurized. Follow site isolation procedures and vent safely before disconnecting any tubing.
Q1) Do you hear or observe valve switching during a run?
  • YES: Go to Q4 (timing or internal valve issue).
  • NO: Go to Q2.
Q2) Is the pneumatic supply (instrument air or valve supply) present and within range?
  • YES: Go to Q3.
  • NO: Restore supply. Check regulator, filter, water trap, and isolation valves, then retry run.
Q3) Are solenoid indicators or diagnostics showing actuation commands?
  • YES: Solenoid or valve actuator may be stuck or faulty. Inspect wiring and service or replace as needed.
  • NO: Method or configuration may not be initiating the valve sequence. Verify method schedule, run state, and interlocks.
Q4) Are peaks missing or incorrect even though valves are switching?
  • YES: Suspect internal valve leak, wrong timing, or contamination → go to Q5.
  • NO: Issue may be intermittent. Check alarm log timing and repeat the run while monitoring valve events.
Q5) Do retention times shift suddenly or do peaks appear in the wrong order?
  • YES: Verify method timing and sequence against the last known good configuration. Restore from backup if needed.
  • NO: Leak test the valve manifold and fittings. Inspect for contamination, oil, or water in pneumatic lines.

Verification

  • Valve events occur consistently in each run.
  • Peak order and retention times return to normal range.
  • Repeatability improves over 2–3 runs.
If still failing:
  • Capture diagnostics: valve event log, method name, and alarm text.
  • Escalate to maintenance or vendor for suspected internal valve leakage or I/O module fault.
