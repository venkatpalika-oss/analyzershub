Valve Actuation / Sequence Fault — Decision Tree

Use when: no injection events, wrong timing, valves not switching, sequence error, pneumatic supply issues.

Safety: Pneumatic lines may be pressurized. Follow isolation rules. Vent safely before disconnecting.
Q1 — Do you hear/observe valve switching during a run?
  • YES: Go to Q4 (timing/leak/internal valve issue).
  • NO: Go to Q2.
Q2 — Is pneumatic supply (instrument air or valve supply) present and within range?
  • YES: Go to Q3.
  • NO: Restore supply, check regulator, filter, water trap, and shut valves → retry run.
Q3 — Are solenoid indicators/diagnostics showing actuation commands?
  • YES: Solenoid may be stuck/faulty or valve actuator stuck → service/replace solenoid/actuator, check wiring.
  • NO: Method/config may not be starting valve sequence → verify method schedule, run state, and interlocks.
Q4 — Are peaks missing/incorrect despite valves switching?
  • YES: Suspect internal valve leak, wrong timing, or contamination → go to Q5.
  • NO: Issue may be intermittent. Check alarm log timing and repeat run while monitoring valve events.
Q5 — Do retention times shift suddenly or peaks appear in wrong order?
  • YES: Verify method timing/sequence vs last known good. Restore method from backup if changed.
  • NO: Leak test valve manifold and fittings. Inspect for contamination/oil/water in pneumatic lines.
Verification:
  • Valve events occur consistently in each run.
  • Peak order and retention time return to normal range.
  • Repeatability improves (2–3 runs).
If still failing:
  • Capture diagnostics: valve event log, method name, alarm text.
  • Escalate: possible internal valve leakage or controller/IO module fault.
