Oven Temperature Fault — Decision Tree

Use this guide when the oven is not ready, temperature is unstable, over/under temperature alarms occur, ramp is slow, or heater / temperature sensor issues are suspected.

Safety: Oven and heater circuits operate at high temperature and voltage. Follow electrical isolation and site safety procedures before touching wiring or components.
Q1) Is the oven temperature close to the setpoint (within acceptable tolerance)?
  • YES: Go to Q2.
  • NO: Go to Q3.
Q2) Is the temperature stable (no oscillation or sudden jumps)?
  • YES: Alarm may be intermittent or historical. Check alarm log time, acknowledge/clear, and monitor for 30 minutes.
  • NO: Suspect sensor drift, loose wiring, door/gasket leak, or controller tuning issue → go to Q4.
Q3) Is the oven heater receiving power (per diagnostics/status)?
  • YES: Go to Q4.
  • NO: Check fuses/breakers, interlocks, heater relay/SSR, and wiring integrity. Restore safely, then recheck.
Q4) Is the temperature sensor reading reasonable (not open/short or extreme value)?
  • YES: Go to Q5.
  • NO: Sensor wiring open/short or sensor failed. Repair wiring or replace sensor, then recheck stability.
Q5) Is there airflow or enclosure heat-loss issue?
  • YES: Inspect oven door seal/gasket, cabinet vents, cooling fans, and drafts. Correct and recheck ramp/stability.
  • NO: Go to Q6.
Q6) Does the method require a specific ramp profile or temperature?
  • YES: Confirm method parameters match the validated configuration or last known good backup.
  • NO / Unknown: Compare with last known good method or restore from backup.

Verification

  • Oven reaches setpoint and remains stable for 15–20 minutes.
  • Retention times return to normal range.
  • Repeat runs show consistent peak shape and retention time.
If still failing:
  • Capture alarm text and oven temperature trend screenshots.
  • Record setpoint vs actual temperature, heater output status, and sensor readings.
  • Escalate to maintenance/vendor for suspected heater element, control board, or sensor circuit failure.
