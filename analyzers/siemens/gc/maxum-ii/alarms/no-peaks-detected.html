No Peaks Detected — Decision Tree

Use this guide when chromatograms show no peaks, flat baseline, missing injections, or zero response across runs.

Field rule: “No peaks” is almost always caused by carrier gas, valve actuation, or detector readiness. Do not change calibration first.
Q1) Is the carrier gas pressure and flow present and stable?
  • YES: Go to Q2.
  • NO: Restore carrier supply, check regulator, filters, and leaks. Re-run analysis after stabilization.
Q2) Are valves switching during the run (audible or in diagnostics)?
  • YES: Go to Q3.
  • NO: Check pneumatic supply, solenoids, and method sequence. Use the Valve Actuation Decision Tree.
Q3) Is the detector “Ready” and baseline visible?
  • YES: Go to Q4.
  • NO: Detector not warmed up, faulted, or saturated. Use the Detector Not Ready Decision Tree.
Q4) Is sample actually reaching the column?
  • YES: Go to Q5.
  • NO: Check sample supply pressure, blocked filters, closed valves, or incorrect sample selection stream.
Q5) Are method parameters correct (injection timing, column assignment)?
  • YES: Go to Q6.
  • NO: Restore last known good method or load backup configuration.
Q6) Is the detector signal connected and scaled correctly?
  • YES: Go to Verification.
  • NO: Check detector wiring, signal path, and channel assignment.

Verification

  • Baseline stable and visible before injection.
  • Peaks appear with expected retention times.
  • Repeatability confirmed over 2–3 runs.
If still no peaks:
  • Capture chromatogram, detector status, and valve event logs.
  • Record carrier pressure, flow, and sample conditions.
  • Escalate for possible column damage, internal valve leakage, or detector electronics fault.
