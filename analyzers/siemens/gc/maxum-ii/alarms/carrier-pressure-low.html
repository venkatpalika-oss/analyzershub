Carrier Pressure / Flow Low — Decision Tree

Use when: no peaks, unstable retention, baseline issues, or carrier supply alarms.

Safety: Carrier gas handling must follow site rules. Check for leaks using approved method. Vent safely.
Q1 — Is the carrier cylinder/regulator pressure adequate?
  • YES: Go to Q2.
  • NO: Replace/restore cylinder supply, open valves fully, set correct regulator outlet pressure → then recheck alarm.
Q2 — Is carrier pressure stable (not hunting/oscillating)?
  • YES: Go to Q3.
  • NO: Suspect regulator creep/failure or restriction. Replace regulator, check filters/restrictors, confirm no icing → recheck.
Q3 — Any audible leak / smell / leak-test positive around fittings?
  • YES: Tighten fittings, replace ferrules, leak test again. Pay attention to valve manifold, detector fittings, column connections.
  • NO: Go to Q4.
Q4 — Is there a blockage/restriction (filters, restrictors, moisture trap)?
  • YES: Replace clogged filters/restrictors, drain traps, check tubing kinks → recheck pressure/flow.
  • NO: Go to Q5.
Q5 — Does the method require a specific carrier and flow setpoint?
  • YES: Confirm correct carrier gas type and setpoint per method. Wrong gas/flow causes bad peaks or no peaks.
  • NO / Unknown: Verify method configuration and compare with last known good backup.
Verification:
  • Carrier pressure stable for at least 10–15 minutes.
  • Baseline stable and retention times return close to normal.
  • Run a repeatability test (2–3 runs) and confirm peaks appear.
If still failing:
  • Capture alarm text + diagnostics screenshots.
  • Record carrier supply pressure, regulator outlet pressure, and any flow readings.
  • Escalate to maintenance/vendor: suspected internal leak, valve block leak, or module fault.
