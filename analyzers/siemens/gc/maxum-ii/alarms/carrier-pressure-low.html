Carrier Pressure / Flow Low — Decision Tree

Use this decision tree when you observe no peaks, unstable retention times, baseline issues, or carrier supply–related alarms on a Siemens MAXUM II GC.

← Back to Alarms MAXUM II Overview →
Safety: Carrier gas handling must follow site safety rules. Leak-test using approved methods only and vent safely.

Q1 — Is the carrier cylinder / regulator pressure adequate?

  • YES: Proceed to Q2.
  • NO: Replace or restore cylinder supply, fully open valves, set correct regulator outlet pressure, then recheck alarm.

Q2 — Is the carrier pressure stable (no hunting or oscillation)?

  • YES: Proceed to Q3.
  • NO: Suspect regulator creep/failure or restriction. Replace regulator, inspect filters/restrictors, check for icing, then recheck pressure.

Q3 — Any audible leak, odor, or positive leak-test result?

  • YES: Tighten fittings, replace ferrules, and leak-test again. Pay special attention to valve manifolds, detector fittings, and column connections.
  • NO: Proceed to Q4.

Q4 — Is there a blockage or restriction?

  • YES: Replace clogged filters or restrictors, drain moisture traps, inspect tubing for kinks, then recheck pressure and flow.
  • NO: Proceed to Q5.

Q5 — Does the method require a specific carrier and flow setpoint?

  • YES: Confirm correct carrier gas type and flow setpoint per method. Incorrect carrier or flow will cause poor or missing peaks.
  • NO / Unknown: Verify method configuration against the last known-good backup.

Verification Checklist

  • Carrier pressure stable for at least 10–15 minutes
  • Baseline stable and retention times close to normal
  • Run 2–3 repeatability cycles and confirm peak presence

If the Problem Persists

  • Capture alarm text and diagnostics screenshots
  • Record carrier supply pressure and regulator outlet pressure
  • Escalate to maintenance or vendor support — suspected internal leak, valve block leak, or module fault
