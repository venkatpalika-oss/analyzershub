Detector Not Ready / Baseline Fault

Use this decision tree when the baseline is missing, noisy, saturated, warm-up does not complete, or detector fault alarms occur.

← Back to Alarms MAXUM II Overview →
Safety: Some MAXUM II detectors operate at high temperature or current. Follow plant safety rules and OEM instructions before opening detector modules.

Q1 — Has the detector completed warm-up / stabilization?

  • Yes: Proceed to Q2
  • No: Allow full warm-up, confirm stable power and no repeated restarts

Q2 — Is carrier gas stable and correct for the method?

  • Yes: Proceed to Q3
  • No: Restore correct carrier type, pressure, and flow

Q3 — What is the baseline condition?

  • Saturated (pegged high/low): Suspect wiring, configuration error, or contamination → Q4
  • Noisy: Suspect flow instability, leaks, grounding issues → Q5

Q4 — Any recent maintenance or configuration changes?

  • Yes: Restore last known good backup, verify detector wiring and method settings
  • No: Inspect for contamination and check detector diagnostics

Q5 — Leak, stability, and noise checks

  • Leak test detector, column, and valve manifold fittings
  • Verify stable pressures (no regulator hunting)
  • Check grounding and cable shielding
  • If issues found → correct and re-evaluate baseline

Q6 — Do detector self-diagnostics show a module fault?

  • Yes: Plan detector module service or replacement
  • No: Perform controlled purge/cleanup per OEM and monitor trend

Verification

  • Baseline stable for at least 10–15 minutes
  • Repeat runs show consistent peak area and retention time
  • No recurring detector alarms after multiple cycles

If Still Failing

  • Capture baseline screenshots and detector diagnostics
  • Record carrier pressure, flow, and sample conditions
  • Escalate to maintenance or vendor — suspected detector or electronics fault
