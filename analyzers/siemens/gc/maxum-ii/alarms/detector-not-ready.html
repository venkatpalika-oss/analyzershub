Detector Not Ready / Baseline Fault — Decision Tree

Use when: no baseline, noisy baseline, saturated signal, warm-up not complete, detector fault/alarm.

Safety: Some detectors use high temperature/current. Follow OEM and plant rules before opening modules.
Q1 — Has the detector completed warm-up / stabilization time?
  • YES: Go to Q2.
  • NO: Allow full warm-up. Confirm stable power and no repeated restarts → recheck baseline.
Q2 — Is carrier gas stable and correct for the method?
  • YES: Go to Q3.
  • NO: Restore carrier pressure/flow and correct gas type → baseline often becomes stable after correction.
Q3 — Is baseline saturated (pegged high/low) or extremely noisy?
  • SATURATED: Suspect wrong wiring/module fault, severe contamination, or wrong flows → go to Q4.
  • NOISY: Suspect flow instability, leaks, grounding/shielding, contamination → go to Q5.
Q4 — Any recent maintenance or configuration change?
  • YES: Recheck detector connections, method settings, and restore last known good configuration/backup.
  • NO: Inspect for contamination in sample path; verify detector module diagnostics for fault codes.
Q5 — Leak test and stability check
  • Leak test around detector/column fittings and valve manifold.
  • Confirm pressures stable (no hunting).
  • Check grounding and cable shielding (noise issues).
  • If leak/noise found: fix leak/grounding → recheck baseline.
  • If no issue found: go to Q6.
Q6 — Does detector self-diagnostics indicate module fault?
  • YES: Plan module service/swap or vendor support.
  • NO: Perform controlled purge/cleanup as per OEM method and monitor baseline trend.
Verification:
  • Baseline stable for 10–15 minutes.
  • Repeat run gives consistent peak area and RT.
  • No recurring detector alarms after 2–3 cycles.
If still failing:
  • Capture diagnostics: baseline screenshot, detector status, alarm text/time.
  • Record carrier pressure/flow and sample conditions.
  • Escalate: suspected detector module failure, contamination, or electronics issue.
