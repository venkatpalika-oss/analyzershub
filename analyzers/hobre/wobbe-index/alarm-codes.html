Hobré Wobbe Index Analyzer — Alarm Codes (Categories)

Quick reference to common alarm groups and first actions for stable Wobbe/CV measurement.

← Back to Analyzer Alarm Decision Trees → Back to Search

Typical Alarm Categories

  • Sample Conditioning – moisture / liquid carryover, filter blockage, drain issues
  • Pressure / Flow – regulator hunting, restrictions, leaks, low supply pressure
  • Temperature / Stability – internal temperature deviation / cabinet overheating
  • Calculation / Quality – unstable output, invalid inputs, wrong scaling to DCS
  • Communication / Output – network, Modbus/OPC, mapping, quality/status mismatch

Major Causes in the Field (Most Common)

  • Liquid carryover from sample line → causes sudden jumps/incorrect Wobbe
  • Moisture / dewpoint issues → unstable reading, sensor drift
  • Pressure hunting at regulator → fluctuating outputs
  • Wrong scaling/mapping → value looks wrong only at DCS

Calibration Notes (Quick)

  • Calibrate only after sample is stable and no conditioning alarms exist.
  • Use correct reference gases/standards as per site procedure.
  • After calibration, verify stability over time and confirm DCS scaling/quality.

Field Best Practice

  • Fix sampling & pressure stability before touching calculation/output settings.
  • After maintenance, verify with stable trend (not just one reading).
  • Compare with lab only after conditioning is verified.
