Working Principle (Technician View)

A Wobbe Index analyzer provides a continuous indication of fuel-gas quality. Wobbe Index helps combustion systems maintain stable heat input because it relates energy flow through a nozzle/orifice at a given pressure.

Calibration Procedure (Field Standard)

Use your site‚Äôs approved method and reference gases/standards. The steps below are a technician sequence to avoid repeated failures and unstable post-calibration output.

1) Stabilize first
Ensure no sampling/pressure alarms. Confirm stable trend locally.
2) Verify flow/pressure
Correct regulator setting, no hunting, filters/drains OK.
3) Apply reference
Apply zero/reference condition and wait for stabilization.
4) Span/verification
Apply span/reference point and verify response and stability.
5) Return to process
Allow reading to settle. Confirm within expected limits.
6) Confirm DCS
Check scaling + quality/status. Record results in calibration log.
Don‚Äôt calibrate if: sample is wet, drains are blocked, pressure is hunting, or readings are not stable. Fix those first or calibration will ‚Äúlook good‚Äù temporarily and fail again.

Major Troubleshoots (Top Problems + Fast Checks)

1) Wobbe value fluctuates
Check regulator hunting, sample moisture/liquid, filter saturation, drains. Use decision tree: Pressure/Flow ‚Üí Sampling.
2) Wobbe wrong vs lab
Confirm stable sample first. Then verify DCS scaling/mapping and any calculation parameters. Compare after stable period (not one moment).
3) Sudden step change
Most likely process composition change or liquid carryover during upset. Inspect drains and conditioning immediately.
4) Calibration fails / won‚Äôt hold
Wrong reference flow/pressure, leaks, wet sample, or unstable trend. Stabilize ‚Üí retry calibration.
5) DCS not updating / bad quality
Verify network/protocol, mapping, scaling and quality bit handling. Use: Comms / DCS.
6) Slow drift over days
Check moisture ingress, filter condition, contamination, temperature drift. Confirm cabinet ventilation and stable ambient conditions.
