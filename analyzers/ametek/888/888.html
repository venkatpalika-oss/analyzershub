AMETEK 888 Tail Gas Analyzer

Continuous tail gas monitoring for sulfur recovery units (SRU), incinerators, and refinery sulfur plants.

Alarm Codes → Decision Trees → Back to Search →

Working Principle

The AMETEK 888 Tail Gas Analyzer is designed for sulfur recovery applications to continuously measure tail gas components such as H₂S, SO₂, and total sulfur.

The analyzer typically uses UV / thermal / electrochemical detection techniques depending on configuration, allowing accurate monitoring of sulfur conversion efficiency and ensuring compliance with environmental regulations.

Main Components

  • Sample probe and heated sample line
  • Sample conditioning system
  • Analyzer module (detector & electronics)
  • Calibration gas solenoid manifold
  • Analyzer controller / HMI
  • Analog & digital communication interfaces

Calibration Procedure

  1. Ensure analyzer is warmed up and in stable operation
  2. Verify sample system temperature and flow
  3. Apply Zero gas (typically nitrogen)
  4. Apply Span gas with certified sulfur concentration
  5. Allow readings to stabilize before accepting calibration
  6. Return analyzer to process and verify normal readings
Field Tip: Frequent calibration drift usually indicates sample line contamination, sulfur deposition, or heater issues.

Major Troubleshooting Areas

  • High Drift – sample conditioning failure, sulfur buildup
  • Calibration Failure – incorrect gas, leaks, regulator instability
  • Low Response – blocked probe, condenser issues
  • Analyzer Offline – power, controller, or I/O fault
  • High Noise – grounding or detector contamination
