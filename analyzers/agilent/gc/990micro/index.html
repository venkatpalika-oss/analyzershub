Agilent 990 Micro GC — Portable Gas Chromatograph

Portable micro gas chromatograph for fast, on-site gas analysis. Used in field surveys, temporary installations, process verification, and rapid troubleshooting.

← Back to Search GC Alarm Decision Trees → Chromatogram Guide →
Field rule: Most 990 Micro GC issues are caused by sample moisture, pressure instability, or ambient temperature effects, not electronics or software.

Working Principle

The Agilent 990 Micro GC separates gas components using micro-machined columns housed in modular analytical channels. Each module performs rapid separation and detection.

  • Fast analysis: short columns, quick cycle times
  • Module-based: each channel operates independently
  • Portable operation: sensitive to environment and sample quality

Accuracy depends heavily on dry sample gas, stable inlet pressure, and consistent ambient temperature.

Main Components (Field Focus)

  • Sample inlet: quick-connect fittings, filters
  • Pressure regulators: inlet pressure control
  • Micro GC modules: columns + detectors
  • Temperature control: module heaters
  • Internal pump / carrier system
  • Controller & software: local PC or embedded interface

Calibration Procedure (Technician Sequence)

1) Power up and allow full thermal stabilization
2) Verify inlet pressure (stable, within limits)
3) Confirm sample gas is dry and filtered
4) Apply certified calibration gas
5) Run multiple cycles to confirm repeatability
6) Save response factors and verify against standard

Tip: If retention time or peak area varies between runs, do not calibrate. Fix pressure or moisture issues first.

Major Troubleshooting (Top Issues)

  • No peaks: blocked inlet, no sample flow, module not active
  • Unstable peaks: moisture, pressure fluctuations
  • Retention time shift: temperature instability, module aging
  • Poor repeatability: regulator hunting, leaks
  • Communication loss: USB / Ethernet / software timeout
