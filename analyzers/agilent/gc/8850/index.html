Agilent 8850 — Gas Chromatograph

Compact GC platform for routine laboratory and industrial gas analysis. Focus: injection stability, carrier gas control, detector repeatability, and basic method robustness.

← Back to Search GC Alarm Decision Trees →
Technician rule: If results drift or repeatability is poor, check carrier pressure, inlet leaks, and injection timing before touching calibration.

Working Principle

The Agilent 8850 separates gas components in a chromatographic column and detects them as peaks using detectors such as FID or TCD.

  • Stable carrier gas flow is critical
  • Injection repeatability defines accuracy
  • Detector stability defines noise and drift

Main Components (Field Focus)

  • Sample inlet, split/splitless system
  • Carrier gas supply and EPC
  • Column oven and temperature control
  • Detector module (FID/TCD)
  • Controller, I/O, and data interface

Calibration Procedure

1) Stabilize oven and detector temperatures
2) Verify carrier pressure and flow
3) Inject calibration standard (2–3 runs)
4) Update response factors
5) Verify repeatability and retention times

Major Troubleshooting

  • No peaks: inlet leak, injection failure, detector off
  • Poor repeatability: pressure hunting, injector contamination
  • Retention shift: oven instability, carrier flow change
