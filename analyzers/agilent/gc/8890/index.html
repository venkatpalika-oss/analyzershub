Agilent 8890 — Advanced Process Gas Chromatograph

High-end GC with advanced diagnostics, automation, and multi-detector capability. Widely used in critical refinery, petrochemical, and analytical applications.

← Back to Agilent GC Alarm Decision Trees →
Technician rule: Advanced diagnostics help, but sampling, carrier gas, and valve stability still cause most GC problems.

Working Principle

The 8890 performs high-precision separation and detection of gas components with enhanced control of temperature, pressure, and detector conditions.

Designed for high repeatability, low drift, and automated diagnostics.

Main Components

  • Advanced inlet & injection control
  • Electronic pressure control (EPC)
  • High-stability oven & thermal management
  • Multiple detector support
  • Controller, diagnostics & networking

Calibration Best Practice

1) Verify EPC stability and pressure readings
2) Confirm clean, stable baseline
3) Run certified standard (multiple injections)
4) Review diagnostics before accepting calibration
5) Verify with independent check gas

Major Troubleshooting Areas

  • Baseline noise: contamination, grounding, detector aging
  • RT drift: EPC drift, oven instability
  • Low response: inlet restriction, detector fault
  • Intermittent alarms: valve timing or pressure hunting
