Agilent 8860 — Flexible Process Gas Chromatograph

Mid-range gas chromatograph supporting multiple detectors and configurations. Commonly used in refinery, petrochemical, and laboratory process support applications.

← Back to Agilent GC Alarm Decision Trees →
Technician rule: When results become unstable, check carrier pressure, injection repeatability, and leaks before touching methods or calibration.

Working Principle

The Agilent 8860 separates gas components in a temperature-controlled column and detects them using standard GC detectors (FID, TCD, etc.).

  • Injection: sample introduced via inlet or valve
  • Separation: components separate in the column
  • Detection: detector converts components to peaks
  • Calculation: peak area → concentration

Main Components (Field Focus)

  • Sample inlet / injection system
  • Carrier gas supply & regulators
  • Oven & temperature controller
  • Column(s) & detector module(s)
  • Electronics, HMI & communications

Calibration Procedure (Recommended)

1) Confirm stable baseline and repeatable cycles
2) Leak-check inlet, column, and fittings
3) Apply certified calibration gas
4) Run 2–3 stable injections
5) Save factors only after repeatability is confirmed

Major Troubleshooting Areas

  • Poor repeatability: inlet issues, pressure instability
  • RT shift: oven temperature drift, carrier flow change
  • No / low response: detector fault, blocked inlet
  • No data to DCS: comms mapping or scaling issues
