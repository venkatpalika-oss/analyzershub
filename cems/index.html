CEMS Hub

Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems — practical guidance for compliance, maintenance, troubleshooting, and performance reliability.

The CEMS Hub is a centralized knowledge area focused on real-world operation of Continuous Emission Monitoring Systems in power plants, refineries, cement plants, steel plants, and industrial facilities.

This section is written from a field technician’s perspective, addressing compliance requirements, analyzer reliability, data quality, and common failure modes.

CEMS System Overview

  • Gas analyzers (SO₂, NOx, CO, CO₂, O₂, HCl, HF)
  • Sample probes and heated sample lines
  • Sample conditioning systems
  • Flow control, pumps, chillers, and filters
  • Calibration systems (zero & span)
  • Data Acquisition & Handling Systems (DAHS)

Common CEMS Analyzer Types

Gas Analyzers

SO₂, NOx, CO, CO₂, O₂ — NDIR, UV, chemiluminescence, zirconia.

Flow Measurement

Pitot, ultrasonic, thermal mass for stack gas flow.

Opacity / Dust

Transmissometer and scatter-based particulate monitoring.

Moisture

Wet/dry basis correction using moisture analyzers.

Common CEMS Issues (Field Reality)

  • Frequent calibration failures
  • Condensation and water carryover
  • Sample line plugging or corrosion
  • Analyzer drift and data validity flags
  • DAHS communication and data gaps

Compliance & Regulatory Focus

CEMS operation is governed by regulatory standards that define installation, calibration, QA/QC, and data reporting.

  • US EPA (40 CFR Part 60 / 75)
  • EU Industrial Emissions Directive (IED)
  • ISO 14181 (QAL1 / QAL2 / QAL3)
  • Local environmental authority requirements

CEMS Best Practices

  • Maintain consistent sample temperature above dew point
  • Verify analyzer zero/span trends weekly
  • Inspect probes and filters routinely
  • Correlate analyzer data with process conditions
  • Document all maintenance and calibration activities
