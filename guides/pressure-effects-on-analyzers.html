Pressure Effects on Process Analyzers

Pressure instability is one of the largest hidden causes of analyzer alarms, drift, and false calibration failures.

Key fact: Most analyzers measure concentration, but pressure changes alter gas density.

Regulator Hunting

  • Caused by undersized or damaged regulators
  • Creates oscillating pressure at analyzer inlet
  • Leads to unstable readings and alarms

Pressure Shock

  • Sudden pressure changes damage sensors
  • Common during startup or manual valve operation
  • Shortens sensor life significantly

Analyzer Sensitivity

  • IR analyzers are highly pressure-sensitive
  • Paramagnetic O₂ affected by flow & pressure
  • GC retention times shift with carrier pressure

Best Practices

  • Use proper pressure regulators (not valves)
  • Vent regulators continuously
  • Maintain constant inlet pressure

Field Symptoms

  • Repeatability poor
  • Calibration never stable
  • Random alarms with no clear cause
Technician rule: If pressure is unstable, ignore analyzer readings — they are physically meaningless.
