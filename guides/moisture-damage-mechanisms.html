Moisture Damage Mechanisms in Analyzers

Moisture is the number one killer of process analyzers. Damage is often invisible until the analyzer becomes unreliable.

Key fact: Once moisture enters an analyzer, damage continues even after drying.

Condensation

  • Occurs when temperature drops below dew point
  • Common during night or winter operation
  • Creates liquid water inside sample lines

Corrosion

  • Moisture reacts with acidic gases
  • Damages sensors, fittings, electronics
  • Corrosion often irreversible

Sensor Damage

  • Paramagnetic O₂ sensors lose stability
  • IR optics become contaminated
  • GC columns permanently affected

Prevention

  • Maintain temperature above dew point
  • Use heat tracing where required
  • Install drains at low points

Field Indicators

  • Slow response time
  • Unstable baseline
  • Frequent recalibration required
Field truth: Most moisture damage is caused by sampling design, not process conditions.
